# Product Strategy

## Positioning

My Wealth should be positioned as a shared wealth operating app for couples and families.

The core promise:

> Make it easier for a couple to see the same financial reality and make calmer money decisions together.

This is intentionally narrower than "personal finance app" or "budget tracker." Competing as a generic household ledger is difficult because mature tools already exist. The wedge is shared household use: living costs, investments, cash flow, and long-term wealth goals in one place.

## Primary User

- A couple or young family managing shared living costs.
- They may not want bank/card auto-sync at first, but they want a trustworthy shared record.
- They care about monthly cash flow, investment progress, and whether they are moving toward a larger asset goal.

## Differentiation

- Couple-first setup and invite flow.
- Household net worth, not only expense tracking.
- Investment and cash-flow views in the same product.
- AI assistance where it saves time: receipt scan, categorization, monthly summaries.
- Clear reports that make money conversations easier.

## Monetization Hypothesis

Free should be useful for solo testing, but paid value should concentrate around shared household operation.

Recommended tiers:

- Free: solo records, basic dashboard, limited history, limited receipt scans.
- Pro: unlimited records, receipt scans, portfolio, monthly cash-flow report, export.
- Family: shared household ledger, partner/family members, shared budgets, wealth goal reports, higher scan allowance.

Avoid unlimited AI features unless usage limits or internal cost controls exist.

## Product Rules

1. Prioritize workflows a couple would use weekly.
2. Every dashboard feature should answer a household question.
3. Manual entry must feel faster than opening a spreadsheet.
4. AI features must save time or produce a report worth discussing.
5. Do not add broad fintech features until shared household retention is proven.

## Validation Plan

Run a two-week real-use test with a couple.

Track:

- Did both people enter or review transactions?
- Did the app reduce friction in money conversations?
- Was receipt scan useful enough to keep using?
- Which dashboard section was actually checked?
- Would they pay 4,900-8,900 KRW/month for shared use?

Success signal:

> The couple checks the app before or during a money conversation without being reminded.
